Solutions

Consider the set \[ S = \omega \cup \power{\omega} \cup \power{\power\omega} \cup \cdots\] and show that it models the Zermelo axioms.

First we show that $\overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ is transitive. Base case: \begin{align*} x \in \overset{0}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) = \omega &\bto x \subseteq \omega \text{ (by the transitivity of $\omega$)} \\ &\bto x \subseteq S. \end{align*} Induction step: Suppose $\overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ is transitive. Since \begin{align*} y \in x \in \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) &\bto y \in x \subseteq \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \\ &\bto y \in \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \\ &\bto y \subseteq \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \text{ (by assumption of transitivity)} \\ &\bto y \in \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega), \end{align*} $\overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ is also transitive. Therefore by induction $\overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ is transitive for all $n\in\omega$. Now we show $S$ is transitive. Since \begin{align*} x \in S &\bto x \in \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \text{ (for some $n\in\omega$)} \\ &\bto x \subseteq \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \text{ (since $\overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ is transitive)} \\ &\bto x \subseteq S \end{align*} we have that $S$ is transitive. Lastly we show that $\overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)\subseteq \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ for all $n\in\omega$. Base case: \begin{align*} x \in \overset{0}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) = \omega &\bto x \subseteq \omega \text{ (by the transitivity of $\omega$)} \\ &\bto x \in \power{\omega} = \overset{1}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \end{align*} Induction step: Suppose the above holds for $n$. \begin{align*} x \in \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) &\bto x \subseteq \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \\ &\bto x \subseteq \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \text{ (since $\overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)\subseteq \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ by assumption)} \\ &\bto x \in \overset{n^{++}}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \end{align*} Therefore by induction $\overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)\subseteq \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ for all $n\in\omega$.

  1. Extentionality: Suppose \[ \forall x \in S (x\in A \iff x\in B) \] for sets $A$ and $B$ in $S$. Now we show that $A = B$. Since \begin{align*} x\in A &\bto x \in S \text{ (since $A\in S$ and $S$ is transitive)} \\ &\bto x \in B \text{ (according to our premise)}, \end{align*} we have $A \subseteq B$. The other direction is isorphic; we conclude $A = B$.
  2. Empty set: Since $\emset = 0 \in \omega$, $\emset \in S$ and so \[ (\exists B \in S)(\forall x \in S)x\not\in B\] as we needed.
  3. Pairing: Suppose $x, y \in S$. This means $x \in \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ and $y \in \overset{m}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$. Since \[ \overset{0}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \subseteq \overset{1}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \subseteq \cdots \] $x, y\in \overset{k}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ when $k$ is the larger among $n$ and $m$. Therefore $\setof{x, y} \in \overset{k^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$, and so $\setof{x,y} \in S$. In other words, \[ (\forall a \in S)(\forall b \in S)(\exists C \in S)(\forall x \in S)[x \in C \iff x = a \lor x = b], \] which is the pairing axiom for $S$.
  4. Union: Suppose $A \in S$. Since \begin{align*} &x \in \bigcup A \in \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \text{ (for some $n\in\omega$)} \\ &\bto x \in y \in A \subseteq \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \text{ (by the transitivity of $\overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$)} \\ &\bto x \in y \in \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \\ &\bto x \in \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \text{ (again by the transitivity)}, \end{align*} we have that $\bigcup A \subseteq \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$, and so $\bigcup A \in \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ which entails $\bigcup A \in S$. Thus we have a set $B$ in $S$ (namely $\bigcup A$), such that for any $x$ in $S$, \[ x\in B \iff (\exists b \in S)x\in b\in B.\]
  5. Power set: Suppose $A \in S$. Then $A \in \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ for some $n\in\omega$. Since \begin{align*} a\subseteq A \in \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) &\bto a \subseteq A \subseteq \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \text{ (by transitivity)} \\ &\bto a \subseteq \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega) \\ &\bto a \in \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega), \end{align*} we have that $\power{A} \subseteq \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ and so $\power{A} \in \overset{n^{++}}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ and so $\power{A} \in S$. Therefore, for any set $A \in S$ there exists a set $B$ (namely $\power{A}$) such that for all $x$ (in $S$ or otherwise) \[ x \in B \iff x\subseteq A. \]
  6. Subset: All subset axioms are true in $S$. For consider any set $c$ in $S$ and any formula $\phi$ not containing $B$. We seek a set $B$ in $S$ such that for any $x \in S$, \[ x \in B \iff x \in c \land \phi^S.\] This tells us exactly what to take for $B$, namely $\setof{x\in c \mid \phi^S}$. Since $B\subseteq c \in S$, we have that $B\subseteq c \subseteq \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ and so $B \in \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ whence $B \in S$ as we needed.
  7. Infinity: Since $\omega\subseteq\omega$, $\omega\in \power{\omega}$ and so $\omega \in S$. Therefore the axiom of infinity is true of $S$.
  8. The axiom of choice (version I) is true in $S$, since any relation $R \in S$, is such that $R \in \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ and so any $f\subseteq R \subseteq \overset{n}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ is such that $f \in \overset{n^+}{\mathcal{P}}(\omega)$ and so $f\in S$. Therefore the subfuction $F$ of $R$ provided by the axiom of choice is in $S$.
  9. The regularity axiom is true in $S$; any nonempty set $A\in S$ has a member $m$ with the least possible rank. By transitivity $m\in S$ and by the ranks $A\cap m = \emset$.
